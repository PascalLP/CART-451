WEEK 3

Question: Crowdsourcing

Answer: Crowdsourcing in the age of internet is now a very accessible and profitable solution to problems where many inputs or a lot of content is needed.
As seen in the article by Jeff Howe, stock images is an field that has exploded due to the rise of crowdsourcing, there is no need to look for proffesional photographers that do this job for a lot more money, since there are libraries that will most likely have what the client is looking for, and for a lot less money.
Another field where crowdsourcing has turned out to be very beneficial is Research & Development for companies. Websites like InnoCentive and NineSigma let companies who are struggling finding solutions to their problems find them with the help of outside a companys team.
Some disadvantages of all of this external content is that a lot of it is low quality or just useless. The more content there is, the harder it is for quality content to be found and stand out from the crowd. It is also the case that people try to make easy money by offering work services on websites but delivering very lazy results and basically just scamming users. This hurts the reputation of the websites and the practice in general.
